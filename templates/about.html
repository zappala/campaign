Tracy and I have been married for 20 years this summer. We have three teenagers: Dominic, Adam, and Emma.

We love living in Cedar Hills! We moved here in 2005 because we wanted to live in a smaller city, with lots of kids around. I particularly like the parks and trails and enjoy the hiking and fishing that is just a few minutes away in American Fork Canyon. I enjoy photography and camping.

I teach Computer Science at BYU, which is my dream job. I run the Internet Research Lab, where we explore what the Internet should do next, and I run the BYU Open Source Lab, where I mentor students on software projects that help make a difference in the world. I am incredibly passionate about genealogy, and recently built the leaf site, which reads your FamilySearch data and helps you to visualize where your family tree need additional work.

During my time in the city, I have volunteered for the Family Festival and served on the Blue Ribbon Recreation Committee, two and a half years on the planning commission, and one year on the city council. I was appointed in August of 2012 to fill the vacancy created when Gary Gygi was appointed mayor. In my time on the council, I have learned a lot about city government, and have particularly learnd the importance of listening and working together.
