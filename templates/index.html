I love Cedar Hills and would be proud to represent you on the City Council. City government is about neighbors working together to create a great place to live.

I believe in:

  • Smart Fiscal Management

    • When I joined the City Council, the city was running a grill at the recreation center and losing money each month. I encouraged us to save money by leasing the space to a private business, and am very happy we were able to bring Sammy's Cafe to Cedar Hills. In addition to good food, the city is guaranteed a small, but reliable income from leasing out the space.

  • Transparency

    • The city should be as open as possible with its finances. I have created a new web site at budget.cedarhills.org that shows 10+ years worth of financial data from the city, with easy-to-read charts and graphs. I will be helping city staff to keep this site updated each year.

  • Family-Oriented Recreation and Events

    • Our city is doing a great job providing numerous outdoor recreation opportunities, both through the city and in partnership with organizations in the area. We need to continue to expand opportunities where possible, including making good use of our new recreation center to offer fitness, karate and other classes.

  • Planning

    • The next four years will be crucial for planning how our city completes its last years of growth. We need to update our parks plan, develop our commercial area, and work with neighboring cities on improved police and fire services. We may also need to plan for pressurized irrigation meters due to dry summers and water shortages, and should be ready to work with the county if there is an opportunity to be involved in a library system.

  • Civility and Consensus

    • Effective planning for our city requires listening to residents and building consensus. We need to continue to use social media and townhall meetings to involve and communicate with residents on these decisions. Above all, we need to maintain a spirit of civility and unity as we work together on these issues.
