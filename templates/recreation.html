Recreation

Summary

  • The city has a vibrant outdoor recreation program. While we should seek to improve or expand these programs where it makes sense, the city should focus primarily on improving indoor recreation, once the basement of the Recreation Center is finished this summer.
  • The city has a plan in place to expand indoor recreation programs in the north part of the basement. The city should utilize town hall meetings and social media to get resident feedback regarding additional options for the south portion of the basement.
  • We have had several well-attended events, such as the Family Christmas Event, the Easter egg hunts and geocaching, and the July pancake breakfast. The city should continue to hold fun events for families to help develop our sense of community, and should enlist volunteer committees to help when practical.

Recreation

Photo courtesy Scott Petersen

The city is going a great job running flag football, tot and youth soccer, youth basketball, and summer golf camps. Lacrosse, baseball, football, cheer, and additional soccer options are available through local organizations. The city also offers karate, Lego League, and indoor fitness classes.

With the recreation center basement being finished this summer, the city will be expanding its indoor recreation classes in the north portion of the basement. We will be partnering with Jill Griffiths, from Tree Fitness, to run numerous indoor fitness classes, such as Zumba and Boot Camp. This is a great arrangement for us, as residents will receive top-notch fitness classes, while also having the potential to make some revenue from renting out the space. The more successful the fitness classes are, the more options residents will have, and the more money the city will make.

The south side of the basement will be finished this summer as well, but the council has intentionally chosen to leave it empty. This will give us a chance to meet with residents to find out what they want in this portion of the basement. Some ideas include fitness equipment, an area with games for teens to hang out, or a small library. I personally would like to have a library in our city. A library would give us the potential to join the North Utah County Library cooperative, which includes Lehi, American Fork, Pleasant Grove, Eagle Mountain, and Highland. We would also be able to provide digital books, giving us a larger selection than our physical space could hold. However, I think it is important that residents choose the direction for the recreation center, rather than the city council following their personal interests. We plan to get resident feedback through a survey and should also hold town hall meetings and use social media.

Events

City-wide events are a great way to build community spirit. The Family Festival has always been a great activity, and this year it was even better due to the hard work of a large force of volunteers. The city organized a new committee to organize the festival this year, and this increased involvement played a huge role in the success of the event. The committee is working toward a permanent date for the festival so that we can plan on a regular time every year.

This past year, we had a number of well-attended events, such as the Family Christmas Event, the Easter egg hunts and geocaching, and the July pancake breakfast. The Youth City Council played a big role in helping with these activities. I hope that in the coming year the city can expand the number of events held, so that we continue to build our community spirit. We should consider forming an events committee to help organize regular activities, with the help of the Youth City Council.
