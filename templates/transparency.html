Transparency

Summary

  • Transparency is one of the most important qualities for government. Residents have a right to know how their tax dollars are being spent.
  • I have worked with some students at BYU to create a new web site at budget.cedarhills.org that shows 10+ years worth of financial data from the city, with easy-to-read charts and graphs. I will be helping city staff to keep this site updated each year.
  • The city, particularly Jenney Rees, did a great job with the State of the City newsletter and social media outreach this year. We need to continue to engage residents and keep everyone informed.

New Budget Web Site

The city should be as open as possible with its finances. Like most cities, we release a detailed budget showing revenue and expenditures for each of the city funds, broken down by category. However, these are released as a spreadsheet, which can be a little difficult to read. The state of the city newsletter does a great job of simplifying the budget by showing some charts, but I think we can do even better this.

Since my appointment to the council, I have been working with some BYU students to create a new site for the city at budget.cedarhills.org. This site allows you to explore all of the detail in our yearly financial statements, using charts and graphs. I have uploaded 10 years of financial statements to the site, and residents can use the site to examine every line in every report during this time.

The home page of the site shows the city's overall finances:

You can then examine each fund and each year in detail, showing both revenue and expenditures:

You can also view trends for each category in a fund:

I will be continuing to develop this web site for the city in the coming year to provide additional features. I want city staff to be able to add comments to each graph and chart, so they can explain the purpose of each fund, transfers between funds, the trends, and any other information that might be confusing to residents.

I think this type of transparency is so important, I am releasing the code as open source software, so that any city can use it for free.

Communication
